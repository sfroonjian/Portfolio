About Me

I am a data and software engineer with a certificate in Data Science/Analytics from Rutgers University and a Bachelor's degree in biology and education from Stockton University. My top skills include Python, SQL, ETL, Tableau, and JavaScript.

After college, I taught science for almost three years. I decided to go into teaching because I love biology and I'm a very interpersonal person, but I felt like I wasn't able to utilize many of my best skills. I wanted a job where I could work with numbers, problem solve, and think analytically. That's when I started to teach myself how to code, and I quickly realized that I wanted to get a job in the tech industry instead. To speed up the process, I completed an accelerated Data Science certification course. I earned an A+ as my overall average, demonstrating my abilitiy to learn quickly.

Although I changed careers, I don't consider my time as a teacher a waste because I gained many valuable skills from it:

  • Presentation skills from delivering scientific instruction daily in a way kids can understand
  • Collaboration skills from working with other teachers/faculty to create lesson plans, reports, and resources
  • Communication skills from diplomatically resolving issues with unhappy parents
  • Organizational skills from keeping track of hundreds of student records
  • Time management skills from submitting lesson/development plans and growth objectives by their deadlines
  • Creativity from designing a years-worth of resources to fit new learning standards and a range of student abilities

These core competency skills, combined with my coding abilities and scientific background, makes me a well-rounded and valuable candidate for any data science position.
